Chapter 4: The Hollow Crown

The man who wore the Hollow Crown was Wren's uncle, Regent Halvard Veyle, who had
ruled Aldercross since the barge went down. Veris laid it out plainly at the
aqueduct: Halvard had paid the bargemen, Halvard had mourned loudest, Halvard had
slipped the crown onto his own head while the kingdom wept.

Wren thought of the glass towers again — that other life that pressed at the edges
of this one, as if some older soul had been poured into the drowned prince and had
never quite settled. Whoever he had been before the floods, he was Caelum now, and
Caelum had a debt.

"I am a thief," he told the Gray Sparrow. "You are asking me to steal a crown."

"I am asking you to steal back what was taken," Veris said. "The Coil taught you
the rest."

Ser Dunmore found them before dawn — but Dunmore, it turned out, had never stopped
serving Maela either. He went to one knee in the wet grass when he saw the heron
ring on the boy's hand. Three of them, then, against a regent and a crown: a thief
who was a prince, a councilor who was a spy, and a captain who remembered.

Wren closed his fist around the ring. "Then let's go home," he said.
