Chapter 1: The Thief of Aldercross

Wren moved through the night markets of Aldercross the way water moves through a
cracked wall — quietly, and into every gap. The boy had no family name that
anyone would admit to, only the one the gutter had given him.

Above the stalls hung the banners of House Veyle, blue herons on grey. Wren
hated herons. He could not have said why; the feeling arrived the way his other
strange feelings did, uninvited. Sometimes, between one heartbeat and the next,
he saw a city that did not exist — glass towers taller than any keep, carriages
that roared without horses, a sky stitched with light. Then it was gone, and he
was only a thief again, cold and hungry under the herons he despised.

[T/N: "Aldercross" keeps the original pun on "alder" and "across"; see glossary.]

He worked for the Coil, the thieves' guild that ran the lower city. Tonight the
Coil wanted a signet ring from a drunk merchant at the Bent Nail tavern. Easy
work. Wren slid two fingers past the man's belt, lifted the ring, and was three
streets away before the merchant called for the watch.

Only later, by candlelight, did Wren look at his prize. The ring bore a heron.
