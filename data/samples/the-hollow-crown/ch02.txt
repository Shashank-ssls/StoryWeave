Chapter 2: The Heron's Heir

The ring should have meant nothing. A merchant's bauble, worth a few coins melted
down. But Wren could not stop staring at the heron, and the hatred he always felt
curdled into something colder: recognition.

He knew this crest. Not from the banners over the market — from somewhere deeper,
a memory that was his and yet felt borrowed. A nursery with blue curtains. A woman
singing. A name spoken over a cradle: Caelum.

Wren was Caelum. The thought did not feel like a discovery so much as a door he had
kept shut. Prince Caelum Veyle, only son of Queen Maela, drowned at six when the
royal barge went down in the Aldercross floods. The whole kingdom had mourned the
little heir. The whole kingdom was wrong. He had not drowned; he had washed up in
the lower city with no memory and a head full of glass towers, and the Coil had
raised him into a thief.

By dawn the watch was hunting a ring-thief in earnest. Their captain, Ser Dunmore,
posted men at every gate. Dunmore had served the old queen once. If he saw Wren's
face in good light, the boy thought, he might see a ghost.
