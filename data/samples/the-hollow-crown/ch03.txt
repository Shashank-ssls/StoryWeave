Chapter 3: The Gray Sparrow

The message reached Wren the way all dangerous messages did — folded inside a loaf
from a baker who would deny ever seeing him. Meet the Gray Sparrow at the old
aqueduct. Come alone.

Everyone in the lower city knew the Gray Sparrow by reputation and no one by face.
The Sparrow traded in secrets the way the Coil traded in coin, and the two often
did business. Wren went, because a thief who ignores the Sparrow does not stay a
thief for long.

At the aqueduct a cloaked figure waited, and when the hood came down Wren found
himself looking at a woman he half-recognized from the upper city: Lady Veris,
who sat on the king's own council and smiled at court like a knife smiling. The
Gray Sparrow and Lady Veris were the same person. The spymistress of the realm
ran her own shadow network under a name no councilor suspected.

"You have your mother's eyes, Caelum," she said, and the use of the name told him
she had known the truth longer than he had. "Maela was my friend. I have waited
eleven years for the heron to come home. The man who sank that barge wears the
Hollow Crown now. Help me, and I will help you take it back."
